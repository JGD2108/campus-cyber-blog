Starting Ethical Hacking at USF

A simple roadmap for USF students curious about cybersecurity and ethical hacking.

What Ethical Hacking Means

Ethical hacking means testing systems with permission to help keep them safe, not to break them. At USF, that might look like working with the CSE Department or the Whitehatters Computer Security Club on approved labs and Capture The Flag events, instead of randomly “testing” campus WiFi or Canvas on your own.

Legal + Ethical Basics

Always remember: permission is key. Engaging in hacking activities without explicit, written authorization is illegal. Ethical hacking operates within a strict framework of rules and a code of conduct. The goal is to improve security, not cause damage. For example, scanning USF’s campus network or trying to “test” Canvas without written approval is not ethical hacking — it’s just illegal access. Familiarize yourself with laws like the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) to understand the legal boundaries.

Quick Start Checklist

  • Sign up for a free TryHackMe account and complete the “Intro” room.
  • Join the USF Whitehatters Discord or mailing list.
  • Pick ONE skill this week (Linux, networking, or Python) and practice for 30 minutes.
  • Turn on MFA for your USF NetID and main email.
  • Set up a virtual machine (e.g., VirtualBox) to create a safe lab environment.
